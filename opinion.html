Opinion is not reporting.
It is reasoning.

The Nautisk covers the news. Opinion is where we — and our contributors — go further. It is where a writer takes a position, makes an argument, and stands behind it with their name.

We do not tell our contributors what to think. We ask only that they think carefully, write honestly, and make a case worth reading. The compass is theirs. The direction is up to them.

Opinion pieces at The Nautisk are not endorsements of The Nautisk's editorial positions. Contributors speak for themselves. We publish perspectives that challenge, provoke, and illuminate — even when we do not agree with every word.

What we will not publish is work that misrepresents facts, attacks individuals without basis, or abandons the basic standards of honest argument. Opinion is a license to be bold. It is not a license to be careless.

If you have something to say — about a law that does not make sense, a market that rewards the wrong people, a health policy that is failing, or a political moment that demands a response — we want to hear from you.

The Nautisk Opinion section is open to contributors from all backgrounds. You do not need to be a professional journalist. You need to have a point of view, a clear argument, and the willingness to put your name on it.

We cover finance, law, healthcare, and politics — but Opinion is freeform. If your piece does not fit a neat category, that is fine. If it does not fit what everyone else is saying, even better.

Send us a pitch — a few sentences describing what you want to argue and why it matters — and we will get back to you.

What we look for

A few things that make a strong Opinion submission.

  • 01

    A clear argument. Not a summary of events — a position. Tell us what you think and why you think it.

  • 02

    Honest reasoning. Make your case with evidence and logic. We welcome bold takes. We do not publish misleading ones.

  • 03

    A length that fits the argument. Most Opinion pieces run between 600 and 1,200 words. Some need more. Do not pad. Do not cut what matters.

  • 04

    Your name on it. We publish named contributors only. Stand behind what you write.

  • 05

    A fresh angle. We are not looking for the take that has already been written a hundred times. Tell us something we have not heard.

Response time: We aim to respond to all pitches within 5 business days. Not every pitch will be accepted, but every one will be read.

Types of pieces we publish

The Argument

A direct case for a position on a policy, decision, or issue. Clear, reasoned, and unafraid to take a side.

The Essay

A longer, more exploratory piece that works through a complex idea without necessarily landing on a single answer.

The Response

A direct reply to something published elsewhere — a law, a ruling, a speech, or another piece of writing worth engaging with.

The Personal

First-person experience that illuminates a larger issue. Your story, connected to a story everyone should understand.

The Critique

A rigorous examination of an idea, institution, or policy — what is wrong with it, why it matters, and what should change.

The Prediction

A reasoned forecast about where something is heading — backed by evidence, not just instinct.
