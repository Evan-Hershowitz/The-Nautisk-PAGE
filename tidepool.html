Corrections Policy

Last updated June 22, 2026

The Nautisk is committed to accuracy in everything we publish. When we make mistakes we correct them promptly, transparently, and without attempting to minimize or obscure the error. This policy explains how we handle corrections, clarifications, and updates to published content.

1. Our commitment

Getting the facts right is the most fundamental obligation of journalism. When we fall short of that standard we owe it to our readers, our sources, and the subjects of our reporting to set the record straight as quickly and clearly as possible. We do not delete articles or silently edit errors without acknowledgment. Every correction is documented and displayed prominently on the affected article.

2. Types of corrections

We distinguish between three types of post-publication changes:

  • A correction addresses a factual error in a published article. Corrections are always clearly labeled and explain what was wrong and what the correct information is.
  • A clarification addresses language or framing that was accurate but potentially misleading. Clarifications are labeled and explain what additional context has been added.
  • An update adds new information to a developing story without correcting an error. Updates are labeled with the date and nature of the new information.

3. How corrections are displayed

Corrections are displayed at the top of the affected article so readers encounter them before reading the content. The correction note includes the date the correction was made and a clear explanation of what was wrong and what the correct information is. We do not simply remove or overwrite incorrect information without acknowledging that a change was made.

Correction June 23, 2026 — An earlier version of this article incorrectly stated that the Federal Reserve raised interest rates by 0.5 percentage points. The correct figure is 0.25 percentage points. We regret the error.

We do not make silent corrections. Any factual change to a published article after its initial publication will be noted with a correction, clarification, or update label as appropriate. This applies to all content on the site regardless of when it was published.
